01 — Voice Identity

The Core Voice

Sync Valuations speaks as music industry veterans who have been in the room where sync deals get made — for 20+ years each, 40+ years combined.

We don't perform expertise. We demonstrate it by explaining complex things simply, backing every claim with data, and never overselling.

"We've negotiated sync deals for over 20 years. Here's what it's worth and why."

The voice is not institutional, consultative, legalese, or startup-speak.

04 — Founders

Two Voices, One Brand

Marcel — Relationship Builder

Conversational confidence. Opens with warmth, moves fast to substance. Frames complexity in one sentence. Uses concrete numbers without hedging. Short paragraphs.

"In other words: we are sync licensing valuation experts."

Nicholas — Methodology Defender

More structured and formal than Marcel, but never cold. Methodical — addresses feedback point by point. Diplomatic firmness. Expert-grade precision. Always provides clear next steps.

Signature Phrases

05 — Combined Voice Rules

Seven Rules

#Rule
1Lead with the answer, then explain. Never build up to a conclusion.
2Use numbers, not adjectives. "73% settlement rate" beats "significantly improved outcomes."
3Short sentences for authority. Long sentences for nuance (methodology sections only).
4Acknowledge, then hold ground. When challenged on methodology, validate the question, then defend the framework.
5No jargon-for-impression. If a term doesn't make the explanation clearer, cut it.
6Independence is structural, not claimed. Don't say "we are independent." Show it: "We serve only one party per case."
7xSV™ is always software, never just methodology. It's systematised expertise, not a framework on paper.

03 — Usage by Context

Which Surface, When

Every layout context has a correct background. Don't mix freely — backgrounds should feel deliberate, not decorative.

Page Hero

Cream or Navy Block

Default hero is plain cream with the geometric shape composition. Navy Block for high-impact campaign heroes only.

Body Sections

Cream / Cream Light / Cream Gradient

Alternate between Cream (#F5F0E3) and Cream Light (#FAF7EF) to create rhythm. Use Cream Gradient on longer scroll sections.

Cards & Modules

Cream Light + Navy Top Rule

Standard cards use Cream Light. Featured / primary card gets Navy Top Rule treatment. The gold variant (featured navy card) is reserved for the primary deliverable only.

Callouts & Quotes

Khaki Left Bar

All pull quotes, key statements, and methodology callouts use the Khaki Left Bar. Never use a coloured background for quotes.

CTA / Inquiry Section

Navy Block

Every page ends with a navy CTA block. Cream text, Khaki Gold for label accents. One per page — never stack two navy sections.

Methodology / Data

Subtle Grid

The grid pattern signals analytical depth. Use once per page, for the section that explains xSV™ scoring logic or data methodology.

Email Headers

Navy Block or Cream

Client-facing emails use Navy Block header (logo in white), cream body. Cold outreach emails are plain cream throughout.

Instagram / Social

Cream, Navy Block, Gold Wash

Carousels: slides alternate Cream and Navy Block. Cover slide uses Gold Wash or Navy. Stories: plain Cream or Navy, never busy backgrounds.

Reports & Documents

Cream Light throughout

PDF reports use Cream Light as the page background. Navy Top Rule for section headers. Never dark backgrounds inside report body.

04 — Rules

Background Do / Don't

Do

Use plain Cream (#F5F0E3) as the default. Start here and add depth only when the layout needs it.

Don't

Use noise textures, photographic backgrounds, or anything that competes with typography. The type is the visual.

Do

Use Navy Block (#1B2C3C) for exactly one high-authority section per page — usually the CTA or hero. It signals importance through contrast.

Don't

Stack two navy sections back-to-back or use navy for more than 20% of a page. The cream-to-navy contrast is what makes navy powerful.

Do

Use Gold Wash for the single most important element on a page — the primary deliverable card, or a key value proposition. One per page.

Don't

Use white (#FFFFFF) as a background. The warmth of Cream is part of the SV identity. Cold white reads as generic and loses the editorial feel.

Do

Test all text on its background for WCAG AA contrast. Deep Navy on Cream passes. Khaki Muted on Cream passes for large text only.

Don't

Use Khaki Gold (#B5A878) as a background fill. It's an accent and border colour — as a background it loses contrast with both cream and navy text.

01 — The Content System

Three Voices. Three Platforms.

Every piece of content flows from one source — the newsletter. LinkedIn and Instagram are the distribution layer, not independent content machines. The newsletter is written once; it then becomes one LinkedIn post, one Instagram Reel script, and one Instagram Carousel. One edition = one week of social content.

"The newsletter is the brain. LinkedIn and Instagram are the megaphones."
VoicePlatformAngleTone
Marcel — Personal LinkedIn + Instagram Founder narrative. The industry, the problem, the journey. Building in public. Warm, direct, conversational confidence. Leads with warmth, moves fast to substance.
Nicholas — Personal LinkedIn Expert depth. Methodology, credibility, the "how we think" angle. Scoring logic. Structured, precise, methodical. Acknowledges then holds ground.
Sync Valuations — Company LinkedIn + Instagram Authoritative brand. Social proof, product results, partnership announcements. The brand voice. Short sentences. Every word earns its place. No jargon.

The strategic logic: Marcel builds the audience, Nicholas builds the trust, SV converts it. All three should feel connected — same palette, same vocabulary, same precision — but distinct in angle.

05 — Instagram Reel

45-Second Script Frame

The highest-reach format. Marcel on camera, phone vertical, natural light, one insight. Lo-fi is correct here — rough edges signal a real person who knows what they're talking about. Write it like explaining something to a friend over drinks. No "hey guys", no "follow for more."

0:00–0:05 — Hook (stop the thumb)

"[Drop straight into the most surprising or counterintuitive thing. No intro. No name. Just the thing.]"

Example: "Most sync infringement cases settle for less than they should. And it's not because the lawyer is bad — it's because nobody established what the music was actually worth."

0:05–0:25 — Context (why this matters)

"[Explain the mechanism. Why does this happen? What's the structure of the problem? Keep it to two or three sentences.]"

Example: "When a track gets used without a license, the instinct is to calculate penalties. But the negotiation that actually happens is about the sync fee — what would a brand have paid to license this track legitimately? And most of the time, no one can answer that question independently."

0:25–0:45 — Punchline (the thing that sticks)

"[Land the insight. One sentence. Make it specific enough to be memorable.]"

Example: "We built xSV™ to answer exactly that question — independently, with 20 years of real deal data behind it. That's what changes the settlement conversation."

ElementGuidance
Caption1–2 sentences max. Restate the punchline, not the whole script. End with a question. Then hashtags: #synclicensing #musicindustry #musiclaw #syncmusic #iplaw
FilmingPhone, vertical (9:16), natural light, no ring light needed. One take or maximum two edits. The roughness is intentional.
